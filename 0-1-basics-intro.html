Maybe you've just learned the rules of riichi mahjong and don't want to just flail around and luck into some wins. Or maybe you've already played quite a bit and want to review the fundamentals. Either way, this is the section for you.[1]

At an extremely reductive level, winning at mahjong consists of three things:

  1. Winning more hands
  2. Winning valuable hands
  3. Not dealing into hands

The first two maximize the points you gain, and the last minimizes the points you lose. Manage all three at once, and you'd do very well for yourself. It's rarely so simple, of course; very often, pursuing one of these goals sacrifices the other two, at least to some extent, and managing these tradeoffs is a key part of being a skilled mahjong player. In any case, if you're a newer mahjong player, becoming proficient in these basic skills is the first step in improving your play, and it's likely that implementing correct strategy can improve all three dimensions at once.

The chapters in this opening section will more or less follow that list. The first and biggest portion is devoted to winning hands as quickly as possible, followed by chapters on maximizing hand value and defensive tactics.[2]

  1. [^] Conversely, these opening chapters will probably be below your level if you already feel confident about your skills, hence why I've numbered it 0.
  2. [^] Here, I'm distinguishing defensive tactics from strategy: the former tells you how to defend, the latter when to defend. In my experience the latter is substantially harder, so for the most part I've opted to save those discussions for later.
